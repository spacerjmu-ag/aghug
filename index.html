SpaceX

Space Exploration Technologies Corp., known as SpaceX, was founded in 2002 by Elon Musk with a bold vision: to revolutionize space technology and make humanity a multiplanetary species. Headquartered at Starbase in Texas, SpaceX has grown from a startup into the world's leading aerospace manufacturer and launch provider, achieving milestones that were once thought impossible.

From the early days of the Falcon 1—the first privately developed liquid-fuel rocket to reach orbit—to the reusable Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy, SpaceX has dramatically reduced the cost of access to space through innovative reusability. The company's Dragon spacecraft became the first commercial vehicle to dock with the International Space Station in 2012, and in 2020, Crew Dragon carried astronauts to the ISS, marking the first crewed orbital launch from American soil in nearly a decade.

Falcon Family: Dominating Orbital Launches

As of late 2025, SpaceX has conducted over 170 Falcon launches this year alone, setting new records for annual launch cadence. Falcon boosters have landed successfully hundreds of times, with many reused dozens of flights, making space access affordable and sustainable.

These launches support NASA missions, national security payloads, commercial satellites, and the ongoing expansion of Starlink.

Starlink: Connecting the World

Starlink has revolutionized global internet access, with over 10,000 satellites launched and thousands in orbit serving millions of users worldwide. By 2025, Starlink provides high-speed connectivity even in the most remote areas, powering education, business, and emergency services.

Starship: The Future of Interplanetary Travel

Starship, the largest and most powerful rocket ever developed, is fully reusable and designed to carry humans and cargo to the Moon, Mars, and beyond. In 2025, SpaceX conducted multiple successful integrated flight tests, including booster catches, advancing rapidly toward operational missions for NASA's Artemis program and Mars colonization.

With massive production scaling at Starbase and new facilities in Florida, Starship is poised to enable humanity's expansion across the solar system.
